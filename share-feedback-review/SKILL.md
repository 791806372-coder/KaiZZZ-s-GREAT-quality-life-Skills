# Share Feedback Review

## Goal

Create a thoughtful private or document-ready review of a daily sharing session. The output should preserve what the classmate shared, name what they did well, and give specific next-step advice.

Default to 300-600 Chinese characters. Be kind, but do not become vague.

## Workflow

1. Identify the main sharer, topic, and intended audience.
2. Separate content quality from delivery quality.
3. Build a full internal review using the rubric below.
4. Choose the points that matter most: 2-3 strengths, 1-2 improvement opportunities, and 2-3 concrete next actions.
5. Write a structured review.
6. Run the final review checklist and remove AI-flavored phrasing.

If the transcript contains multiple speakers, focus on the main presenter and use host comments only as supporting evidence.

## Review Rubric

Content dimensions:

- Topic value: freshness, usefulness, relevance, and audience interest.
- Angle: whether the speaker has a clear point of view.
- Structure: opening, progression, transitions, and closing.
- Evidence: data, cases, demos, screenshots, research, product experience, or personal observation.
- Depth: whether the share goes beyond surface introduction.
- Comparison: whether similar tools, products, industries, or alternatives are compared.
- Practicality: whether listeners can reuse a method, framework, workflow, or source.
- Risk sense: whether the speaker notices limits, compliance, safety, uncertainty, or edge cases.

Delivery dimensions:

- Fluency, pacing, confidence, clarity, plain-language explanation, audience engagement, time control, Q&A handling, and material quality.

Optional dimensions:

- Original observation, story sense, analogy, business insight, technical understanding, frontline experience, humor, personal enthusiasm, and closing strength.

## Output Format

Default format:

```text
分享复盘

内容总结：...

表现亮点：...

可以提升：...

下次建议：...
```

Keep each section concise. "可以提升" should name the issue without sounding harsh. "下次建议" should be actionable.

## Writing Guidance

Make the review specific:

- Mention actual topics, tools, examples, numbers, cases, or arguments from the share.
- Explain why a strength worked.
- Give next actions the sharer can use next time.

Examples of useful advice:

- "补一页同类工具横向对比，让大家更容易判断它适合什么场景。"
- "开头可以先抛一个具体问题，再进入工具介绍，听众会更快进入状态。"
- "数据点很好，后面可以标一下来源和时间，避免前沿话题被追问时显得不稳。"
- "如果有实操演示，可以把关键步骤压成 3-5 步，减少听众记忆压力。"

Avoid vague advice:

- "继续提升表达能力"
- "加强专业深度"
- "丰富内容维度"
- "增强互动性"

Use those only if you immediately explain how.

## Tone Rules

Write like a serious but friendly mentor. It can be more direct than group feedback, but still respectful.

Avoid AI-flavored patterns:

- "不仅...而且..."
- "不是...而是..."
- "综上所述"
- "值得注意的是"
- "整体来看"
- "进一步提升专业深度"
- "充分体现了"
- Repeated three-word praise chains.

Prefer plain language:

- "这个选题挺适合大家"
- "这块讲得扎实"
- "这里还可以再往前走一步"
- "下次最值得补的是..."
- "这个建议不大，但会明显加分"

## Final Review

Before final output, check:

- Is the content summary accurate and not too long?
- Are content and delivery reviewed separately?
- Are the strengths tied to evidence from the share?
- Are improvement points limited to the most important 1-2?
- Are next actions concrete enough to execute?
- Did you remove formulaic AI wording?
- Does the tone feel helpful rather than judgmental?
